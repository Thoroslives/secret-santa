# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Two families (the organiser's own and the extended family), adults across a wide range of ages and comfort with technology, plus one organiser who runs everything. Participants arrive through a personal link a handful of times a year, usually on a phone, to add a short wishlist and, once the draw is done, to see who they are buying for. The organiser adds people, generates and checks the draw, and sends it out.

The defining constraint is **low frequency**: people touch this once or twice a Christmas, so it has to be instantly obvious every time, with nothing to relearn from one year to the next.

The public landing is the only surface that speaks to a first-time arriver; it is treated as a brand surface. Everything behind sign-in (the participant wishlist, the admin dashboard) is a tool and is treated as product.

## Product Purpose

A private, self-hosted Secret Santa for these two families. It replaces public gift-exchange websites with something the family owns: no accounts to create, no ads, no upsell, and the data stays on the family server. Success is simple and total: every participant can open their link, add a wishlist, and find their match with no confusion, and the organiser can run a year's draw in a couple of minutes.

## Positioning

The warmth of a family tradition, not the friction of a product.

## Brand Personality

Warm, crafted, intimate. It should feel like a hand-addressed card, not an app notification: cozy and grown-up, quietly festive, never loud. The voice is friendly and plain, a person talking to family, never marketing copy.

## Anti-references

Generic AI-Christmas: snow overlays, glossy gradients, emoji used as decoration, the reflexive red-and-green that every generator reaches for. Childish or cartoon holiday clip-art: cartoon Santas, kid-party energy. Also being removed from the forked original: the commercial gift-exchange-SaaS framing (pricing, "sign up free", SEO landing copy) and the santa-red kitsch it shipped with.

## Design Principles

Made for us, not sold to us. Every surface reads as a private family thing; nothing on it tries to convert or sell.

Instantly obvious, once a year. The people using this forget it between Decembers, so the next step is always the clear one and no screen needs relearning.

Warmth from craft, not decoration. The cozy feeling comes from type, colour, spacing and small considered details, never from snow, gradients, or emoji.

Quiet, then a moment. Most screens stay calm and legible; the one deliberate flourish is saved for the reveal, when a participant sees their match.

The landing is a doorway, not a pitch. The public page welcomes and signs you in. It never explains features or sells the idea.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Mixed ages and eyesight mean legibility comes first: WCAG AA as the floor, reaching AAA on body text where it is feasible. Generous tap targets for phone use, never colour as the only signal, and a full reduced-motion alternative for every animation. It should work on an older phone in a bright kitchen.
